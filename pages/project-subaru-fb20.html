Overview

Project snapshot, problem and role

Problem

Oil leak / engine sealing issue

The vehicle presented an oil leak / sealing issue that required more than a superficial repair. The project focused on understanding the mechanical condition, accessing the affected sealing areas, and rebuilding the top-end assembly with careful attention to cleanliness, alignment, and torque procedure.

My role

Hands-on mechanical execution

I performed the hands-on work as a personal engineering project: engine disassembly, cylinder head removal, valve and head cleaning, head gasket replacement, timing chain installation, torque-controlled reassembly, diagnostic checking, and startup verification.

Recruiter relevance

Evidence of mechanical discipline

This project demonstrates practical mechanical troubleshooting, structured teardown, assembly discipline, documentation habits, and validation thinking. It is not presented as professional automotive employment, but as evidence of hands-on engineering capability.

Engineering process

From symptom to verified startup

Process followed

  • Started from the visible symptom: oil leak / engine sealing issue.
  • Performed diagnostic checking and visual inspection before expanding the repair scope.
  • Disassembled the engine components required to access the cylinder heads.
  • Removed the cylinder heads on the FB20 boxer engine configuration.
  • Cleaned cylinder heads, valves, and accessible sealing surfaces before reassembly.
  • Replaced the head gaskets and prepared the engine for controlled reassembly.
  • Installed the timing chain and verified cam/crank timing alignment before startup.
  • Used torque procedure discipline during reassembly to reduce the risk of repeat sealing issues.
  • Completed reassembly, diagnostic checking, and successful startup.

Engineering focus

  • Root-cause-oriented troubleshooting instead of replacing parts blindly.
  • Cleanliness and surface preparation for sealing reliability.
  • Fastener control, torque sequence discipline, and assembly verification.
  • Timing alignment as a critical pre-start validation step.
  • Documentation through photos and staged work progress.
  • Translating a personal mechanical project into engineering evidence.
Results / validation

What was verified

Result

Successful reassembly and startup

The engine was reassembled after cylinder head service, head gasket replacement, timing chain installation, and torque-controlled assembly. The engine started successfully after the intervention.

Validation

Post-assembly checks

Validation was limited to practical post-assembly checks: diagnostic checking, startup behavior, visual inspection, and follow-up observation. No performance numbers or measured before/after data are claimed.

Learning

Applicable engineering habits

The main value was not only the repair itself, but the process: controlled teardown, careful inspection, mechanical cleanliness, torque discipline, timing verification, and clear documentation of the work performed.
