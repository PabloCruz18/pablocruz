Overview

Why, how and what we proved

Why

Make production feasible, stable, and auditable

Field/test engineering is not just fixing problems — it’s building systems that stay reliable under real-world constraints. ADS needed a complete manufacturing proposal from scratch: choose the best process/material, predict failure modes, and prove that the measurement system and process controls can maintain GD&T requirements at high volume.

How

Concurrent engineering: process + quality + physical fixture

  • Compared materials and processes (conventional machining, CNC, sand casting, silicone mold casting, optional additive) considering accuracy, finish, cost, and risks.
  • Selected CNC machining + Al 6061 as the best fit for precision, throughput, and tool wear behavior.
  • Designed a high-speed Checking Fixture based on the part’s datums, enabling fast and repeatable placement to “zero”.
  • Integrated metrology: LVDT for critical deformation/flatness (alabeo) and laser for length features (plus vernier/micrometer methods for comparison).
  • Manufactured the fixture (CAD/CAM + CNC milling) and defined a torque-based validation approach to prevent loosening and loss of calibration.
  • Validated measurement and stability using MSA (R&R), SPC (X/R, histograms, Cp/Cpk), and AMEF/FMEA (RPN target < 200).
  • Paperless reporting using QDA X and DataMetrics (traceability: date, time, operator, sample ID; plus CSV import workflow).
Results

Production-ready recommendation with measured capability

  • Final recommendation: CNC machining in Aluminum 6061 as the most feasible and robust path.
  • Projected annual volume (with scrap): 51,515 parts/year (to meet 50,000 good parts/year, assuming 1% scrap per 3 operations).
  • OEE assumption: 0.75 based on 2 shifts and 250 working days/year.
  • Checking Fixture BOM: $2,254 MXN (materials only).
  • Best measurement performance: LVDT for alabeo showed very low GRR% (e.g., ~1% in CNC Al dataset), supporting repeatable verification.
  • Risk reduction: AMEF/FMEA included countermeasures such as a PLC-based pressure check (barometers monitoring clamp pressure) to prevent pneumatic clamping failures.

What I personally owned (hands-on engineering)

  • Led the fixture concept from requirements → datum strategy → layout.
  • Integrated measurement devices (LVDT/laser) into a practical, repeatable inspection flow.
  • Built/manufactured the fixture using CNC milling and assembly discipline.
  • Defined a torque audit mindset to avoid loosening and long-term drift (fixture reliability).
  • Worked across multiple manufacturing options (machining, casting, additive) and learned real tradeoffs.

Why this is relevant for Field / Test / R&D roles

  • Field engineering: diagnose variability, stabilize the process, and validate the system before release.
  • Test engineering: build fixtures, define acceptance criteria, run studies (R&R / SPC), and prove capability.
  • R&D mindset: compare options with evidence, manage risk (FMEA), and convert requirements into hardware.
  • Beyond “technical”: ties design intent (GD&T) to real manufacturing, measurement, traceability, and reliability.
