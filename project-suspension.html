Overview

Why, how and what I got from this project

Why

Go beyond “it runs”

I wanted to understand what actually happens inside an engine over time: how components wear, which mistakes during assembly lead to failures and what good practices look like in real hardware, not just theory.

How

Structured teardown & rebuild

I performed a full teardown, cleaning and inspection of all components, documenting findings with photos, measuring key parts where possible, replacing critical elements and reassembling the engine following torque sequences and a disciplined workflow.

Results

Reliable start & clear learnings

The engine ran again with reduced noise and more consistent behavior. More importantly, I left with a clearer mental model of what “good assembly” means and a list of improvements for tooling, measurement and process for the next build.

Process highlights

  • Planned teardown with labeling and photo documentation.
  • Visual and basic dimensional inspection of key components.
  • Cleaning, degreasing and surface preparation before assembly.
  • Use of assembly lube, correct torque specs and sequences.
  • First start, leak checks and basic functional tests.

Engineering takeaways

  • Importance of cleanliness and organization in reliability.
  • How small assembly errors can create big future problems.
  • Better understanding of wear patterns and what they tell you.
  • How to document a build so it is useful as a portfolio piece.
