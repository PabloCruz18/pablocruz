Overview

Professional automation troubleshooting case study

Field challenge

Keeping mobile automation reliable

LGVs depend on several integrated subsystems working together: navigation, sensing, safety, power, charging, vehicle mechanics, control interfaces, and transfer points. A fault in one area can stop vehicle movement, interrupt production flow, or prevent safe operation.

My role

Field diagnostics and recovery support

I supported field troubleshooting, inspection, maintenance, recovery actions, documentation, and operational checks. My contribution was focused on identifying symptoms, isolating likely failure areas, coordinating corrective work, and validating equipment behavior after intervention.

Outcome

Defensible return-to-operation validation

The value of this work was not just getting a vehicle moving again. The important part was confirming that the issue was understood, the corrective action was reasonable, and the machine could return to operation with safety devices, sensors, movement, and transfer interfaces behaving as expected.

Problem / field challenge

In automated industrial environments, LGV issues can appear as navigation faults, blocked routes, safety stops, charging problems, transfer failures, sensor errors, communication interruptions, or vehicle recovery events. The challenge is to diagnose the real source of the symptom without assuming that the visible alarm is the root cause.

  • Separate mechanical, electrical, sensor, power, safety, and control-interface symptoms.
  • Support troubleshooting while respecting production, safety, and maintenance constraints.
  • Document findings clearly enough for handover, escalation, or future troubleshooting.

My role

  • Performed field inspection and troubleshooting support on LGV / AGV systems.
  • Checked visible mechanical, electrical, sensor, and safety-related conditions.
  • Interacted with PLC-based systems and diagnostic information without overstating programming ownership.
  • Supported preventive and corrective maintenance activities.
  • Validated equipment behavior after corrective actions before considering the issue closed.
  • Communicated findings through technical notes, shift handovers, or support documentation.

Systems involved

  • LGVs / AGVs used in automated warehouse or production environments.
  • LiDAR / PLS scanners, safety sensors, proximity sensors, and vehicle detection devices.
  • Navigation and mapping-related troubleshooting areas, without exposing layouts or proprietary data.
  • Battery systems, charging areas, and power-related checks.
  • Mechanical assemblies, drive components, wheels, conveyors, and transfer interfaces.
  • PLC interaction / diagnostics, I/O status checks, alarms, and machine-state verification.
  • Safety devices, guarded areas, emergency stops, and controlled recovery procedures.

Troubleshooting approach

  • Started from the symptom: alarm, stopped vehicle, failed transfer, charging issue, or abnormal movement behavior.
  • Checked basic conditions first: power, battery state, physical obstruction, safety status, and visible damage.
  • Compared sensor status, scanner behavior, I/O signals, and machine state to the expected operating condition.
  • Isolated whether the likely cause was mechanical, electrical, sensor-related, communication-related, or process/interface-related.
  • Supported corrective actions within field scope and escalated issues when deeper controls, software, or hardware intervention was required.
  • Recorded observations, actions taken, and remaining risks for maintenance or engineering follow-up.

Operational validation / return-to-operation checks

  • Verified that alarms or fault conditions were cleared through proper procedure, not bypassed without understanding.
  • Confirmed safe vehicle movement in the affected area or route segment.
  • Checked sensor and safety-device response where relevant to the issue.
  • Observed charging, transfer, or interface behavior when the fault involved battery or conveyor interaction.
  • Monitored the machine after corrective action to confirm the symptom did not immediately return.
  • Documented the final condition and any recommended follow-up actions.

Results / professional value

  • Demonstrated practical troubleshooting across integrated electromechanical automation systems.
  • Built stronger judgment for separating root causes from secondary symptoms.
  • Improved ability to communicate technical findings in customer-facing and production environments.
  • Practiced safe return-to-operation thinking instead of treating fault clearing as the final step.
  • Gained experience that directly supports field service, automation, maintenance, reliability, and validation roles.
